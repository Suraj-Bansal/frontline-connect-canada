Make Impact. Donate Now


We are currently accepting smartphones and tablets that are funtional with iOS 10.0 or Android v4.4 at minimum. Devices must be in good working order. Donated phones must be unlocked and able to join any cellular network.Read below for more information!

Have an eligible device? Making your donation is as simple as three quick steps!

  1. 1) Complete the Google Form below
  2. 2) Prep your device(s) for donation by following a simple factory reset and disinfection protocol. We will forward these protocols following your form submission.
  3. 3) You will be matched with a nearby facility to drop off your donation. If you are unable to deliver your device, don’t worry! We will connect you with a volunteer who can collect your device from your doorstep.

Criteria for Device Eligibility

Firstly, we are looking for devices that are in good working order. Our requirements are in place to ensure that each device is capable of running the communications apps that we will be loading, such as Zoom, Skype, and Google Duo. Most devices that are in good condition and less than 4 years old will be eligible for donation.

Donated phones must be unlocked. Most devices bought after December 1st, 2017 should be unlocked already, but there are tutorials online that can confirm the process. We have linked the following tutorial for your perusal.

Why can't Frontline Connect Accept Devices that are Locked into Certain Carriers? What if I'm Interested in Donating, but my Phone is Locked?

We have partnered with some phone companies who are providing us with SIM cards for our donated devices. These SIM cards can only be used by phones that are not locked to another carrier.

If your device is locked to your carrier, you may call your carrier and request they unlock it. Per the Canadian Wireless Code, section F.1.ii, your service provider must unlock your device for free upon your request.

My Device is Slightly Damaged. Can you Still Accept it?

We are currently only accepting devices that are in good working order. These devices are going to healthcare providers and very sick, or elderly patients, and thus damage such as a broken volume button or a cracked screen could significantly impair their ability to use their communications apps.

We will not accept devices with:

  1. — Malfunctioning buttons, cameras or microphones
  2. — Issues with charging and battery life
  3. — Cracked/broken screens (even minor cracks prevent proper disinfection protocol

We will accept devices with:

  1. — Slight dents
  2. — Reduced battery life, but still usable for at least a couple hours

How will you Connect me with Nearby Facilities?

We will match you with the closest facility that needs devices using your postal code. Facilities will be within a 30 minute drive at most – if there are none within a 30 minute drive, we will give you the option to donate to a further location, or you may hold onto your device in case another neighbourhood facility requests devices.

Will I Receive a Tax Receipt?

While we are working on tax receipts, we are currently unable to provide them for small donations. We have collaborated with a registered charity for donations of over 25 high-quality devices. If you are making a donation of similar nature, please email us directly at donations@frontlineconnect.ca.
